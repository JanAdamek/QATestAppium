What information is collected?

We collect only personally identifiable information that is specifically and voluntarily provided by you to the Portal. “Personal data” means information about you and/or third parties (where required by law, a third party could be a legal entity represented by you e.g. where you submit data to us about your employer or a client company).

Our collection of personal data will be limited to the information required to provide access to the information stored within the Portal. Such information may include your name, title, company address, email, telephone and fax numbers. This information may be collected either:

  • Directly from you. Where you are asked to provide data such as when you register, use any “Contact Us” features of the Portal; or
  • Indirectly, such as using cookies (as described below) or customizing software to tailor the Portal to your preferences or to track your use of certain pages of the Portal (e.g. your internet protocol address) and other technical means to monitor your use of the Portal or from third parties who provide us with information about you.

It is PricewaterhouseCoopers’ policy to limit the information collected to only the minimum information required to complete your request. We do not generally seek to collect sensitive data through this Portal. If we do seek to collect such data we will ask you to consent to the uses we propose to make of the data. By providing us with unsolicited sensitive data you consent to our using those data, subject to applicable law, for the purposes set out below.

If you are acting as an intermediary or otherwise on behalf of a third party or supply us with information regarding a third party (such as your employer or a client company), you undertake that you are an authorized representative or agent of such third party and that you have obtained all necessary consents from such third party to the collection, processing, use and disclosure by us of their personal data. Because we are collecting the third party’s data from you, you undertake to make the third party aware of all matters listed in this Privacy Statement, preferably by distributing a copy of this statement to them or by referring them to this Portal.

How will personal data be used?

When you visit the Portal your personal data may be collected and processed to allow you to:

  • Upload, update, review and analyze data contained within the site;
  • Contact us for further information.

Your user activity will also be collected to:

  • Administer the Portal for authentication each time the Portal is accessed
  • Operate the Portal to monitor performance and activity statistics in the aggregate (log files of your activity are maintained within Portal and are retained for the minimum period necessary for our website operations. Log files are maintained to validate the integrity of each transaction and are kept confidential for normal operations unless required, in case of a dispute or as required by law) and to assist in maintaining the performance of the Portal

Information attained by the Portal is used only for the intended purpose stated at the time that the information is collected. Unless specifically requested by you, PricewaterhouseCoopers will not use personal data collected from the Portal to facilitate unsolicited marketing activities.

PwC is committed to providing reasonable and practical access to users to allow them the opportunity to identify and correct the accuracy of personal information previously submitted to PwC. When personal information is retained, PwC assumes responsibility for keeping an accurate record of the information once a user has submitted and verified the data. PwC does not assume responsibility for verifying the ongoing accuracy of the content of personal information. When practically possible, if PwC is informed that any personal information collected through insidetaxpolicy.pwc.com is no longer accurate, PwC will make appropriate corrections based on the updated information that you provide.

If you would like to exercise your rights under applicable law to obtain details of personal information PwC holds about you, or to correct that information, please contact the PwC Engagement team listed within the “Team” page of the Portal. You can also contact the PwC Engagement team if you have a complaint about PwC’s handling of your personal information.

Personal data collected by PwC may be transferred or disclosed to third party contractors, subcontractors, subsidiaries and/or other PwC firms, including those outside the US, for the purposes for which the visitor has submitted the information and for the administration of our system or site and/or other internal, administrative purposes. By submitting data on PwC’s website, the visitor is providing explicit consent to the transfer of such data for the fulfillment of his or her voluntary requests or otherwise as set out in the ‘How will personal data be used’ section. Personal data will also, subject to applicable law, be disclosed:

  • To other entities within the PricewaterhouseCoopers network which may follow the privacy practices in this policy or notify you of their own privacy practices;

  • Where such disclosure is required by law through subpoena, search warrant or other forms of legal process.

We do not collect or compile personally identifying information for dissemination or sale to outside parties for consumer marketing purposes, or host mailings on behalf of third parties.

How long is personal data retained?

We will keep your personal data only as long required for the purposes described in this Privacy Statement, or longer if required by applicable law or regulation.. Contact information about visitors (e.g. information generated though registration for access to areas on the Portal) will be kept as long as the information is required to completely service the contact request or until a user requests that we delete that information.

How is personal data secured?

We have implemented generally accepted standards of technology and operational security in order to protect personal information from loss, misuse, alteration or destruction. Only authorized personnel are provided access to personally identifiable information and these employees have agreed to ensure confidentiality of this information.

Are cookies used on the website?

Cookies may be used on some pages of our site. “Cookies” are small text files placed on your hard drive to assist in providing a more customized Portal experience. For example, a cookie can be used to store registration information in an area of the Portal so that a user does not need to re-enter it on subsequent visits to that area. It is PricewaterhouseCoopers’ policy to use cookies to make the navigation of this Portal easier for visitors and to facilitate efficient registration procedures. Site statistics are compiled by third parties, and therefore your IP address will be passed to third parties for statistical reporting only.

After termination of the visit to the Portal, you can always delete the cookie from your system if you wish.

In order to properly manage the Portal we may pass log information on to our operational systems, and identify categories of visitors by items such as domains and browser types. These statistics are reported in the aggregate to our webmasters. This is to ensure that the Portal presents the best web experience for visitors and is an effective information resource.
